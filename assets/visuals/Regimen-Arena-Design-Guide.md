# **1. One-Page Condensed Style Sheet**

## **Regimen Arena Visual Style Sheet**

**Core identity:** Regimen Arena uses clinically grounded tactical character design. Antibiotics are mechanism-based agents, bacteria are recognizable organism-like threat tokens, and resistance/toxicity elements are symbolic counters.

## **Visual Rule**

Every visual element should teach at least one thing: 

- mechanism of action
- organism identity
- resistance pattern
- monitoring requirement
- toxicity risk
- stewardship decision

If it does not teach something, simplify it or remove it.

## **Tone**

Use: 

- tactical
- clean
- memorable
- professionally playful
- clinically meaningful

Avoid: 

- childish mascots
- random fantasy tropes
- generic pills/tablets/IV bags
- “antibiotic Pokémon”
- visuals that imply incorrect mechanisms

## **Visual Hierarchy**

| **Category** | **Style** | **Personality Level** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Antibiotics | Anthropomorphized mechanism-based heroes | High |
| Bacteria | Morphology-first organism tokens | Low |
| Resistance mechanisms | Obstacles, counters, traps, shields, enzymes | Medium |
| Toxicity/labs/status | Clean UI glyphs and warning icons | Very low |

## **Antibiotic Design Rules**

Antibiotics should feel like player-controlled tools or agents.

They may have: 

- armor, tools, weapons, emblems
- class-coded silhouettes
- mechanism-based props
- heroic or anti-heroic personalities

They should not primarily look like: 

- pills
- capsules
- IV bags
- syringes
- literal chemical structures

## **Bacteria Design Rules**

Bacteria should stay recognizable as organisms first.

They should have: 

- organism-specific morphology
- resistance/susceptibility badges
- mild threat cues
- limited anthropomorphism

Avoid: 

- arms and legs
- big expressive faces
- full monster bodies
- excessive cuteness

## **Color Logic**

| **Meaning** | **Color Direction** |
| --- | --- |

| Resistance | Red or purple |
| Antibiotic action | Blue, white, gold |

| Danger / toxicity | Red |
| Neutral clinical UI | Slate / gray |

| Gram-negative rods | Green/teal |

## **Art Tiers**

| **Tier** | **Use** |
| --- | --- |

| Small icon/badge | Status effects, resistance, labs, UI markers |

## **Guiding Sentence**

**The art should make the clinical idea easier to remember, not make the clinical idea fight through decorative fog.**

# **Regimen Arena Sprite & Visual Style Guide**

## **Project Purpose**

Regimen Arena is a tactical antimicrobial stewardship simulator where learners practice choosing, adjusting, narrowing, and monitoring antimicrobial regimens as infection scenarios evolve.

The visual system should support clinical reasoning by making mechanisms, resistance patterns, organism identity, and monitoring needs easier to understand and remember.

## **Core Visual Philosophy**

Every sprite or icon should teach something.

A visual element should communicate at least one of the following: 

- Mechanism of action
- Organism identity
- Resistance mechanism
- Spectrum or susceptibility
- Toxicity / monitoring requirement
- Stewardship principle
- Clinical status

If a visual element exists only because it looks cool, it should be simplified or removed.

## **Tone**

### **The visual tone should feel:**

- Tactical
- Mechanism-based
- Clean
- Memorable
- Professionally playful
- Appropriate for pharmacy, medical, and provider education

### **The visual tone should avoid:**

- Childish mascot design
- Overly cute monster collecting aesthetics
- Generic fantasy characters with no clinical meaning
- Pills/tablets/IV bags as the main drug identity
- Random decoration that does not reinforce learning

## **Visual Hierarchy**

## **Antibiotics**

### **Role**

Antibiotics are the protagonists of the system. They represent tools the learner uses to solve clinical problems.

### **Style**

Antibiotics can be anthropomorphized and character-like.

They may use: 

- Armor
- Weapons
- Tools
- Emblems
- Class-based silhouettes
- Heroic or anti-heroic design language

### **Educational Purpose**

Each antibiotic should visually suggest its class, mechanism, clinical role, or monitoring burden.

### **Examples**

| **Drug** | **Visual Direction** |
| --- | --- |
| Cefazolin | Frontline beta-lactam wall-breaker |
| Vancomycin | Heavy binding sentinel / monitored anti-hero |
| Daptomycin | Calcium-charged membrane striker |
| Meropenem | Elite broad-spectrum breacher |
| Cefepime | Advanced beta-lactam lancer / precision wall disruptor |
| Piperacillin-tazobactam | Partner unit:  beta-lactam attacker plus inhibitor support |

## **Bacteria**

### **Role**

Bacteria are the clinical target or problem. They should be recognizable and clinically meaningful, but not the emotional stars of the game.

### **Style**

Bacteria should be organism-like rather than fully anthropomorphized.

Use: 

- morphology-first design
- simple resistance/susceptibility markers
- mild threat cues
- consistent organism family shapes

Avoid: 

- humanoid bodies
- big expressive faces
- excessive cuteness
- Pokémon-like monster identity

### **Examples**

| **Organism** | **Visual Direction** |
| --- | --- |
| MSSA | Golden cocci cluster + blue S susceptibility badge |
| MRSA | Golden cocci cluster + red/purple resistance marker |
| E. coli | Rod-shaped gram-negative token |
| ESBL E. coli | Rod-shaped token + enzyme/resistance badge |
| Pseudomonas | Teal/blue-green rod with tougher, more evasive visual language |
| VRE | Enterococcus-like paired/clustered organism + Van resistance marker |

## **Resistance Mechanisms**

### **Role**

Resistance mechanisms explain why therapy fails or becomes complicated.

### **Style**

Resistance elements may be symbolic or semi-characterized.

They should feel like: 

- counters
- obstacles
- traps
- altered targets
- enzymes
- shields
- locks
- decoys

### **Examples**

| **Mechanism** | **Visual Metaphor** |
| --- | --- |
| Beta-lactamase | Cutting enzyme / disabling countermeasure |
| PBP2a | Altered lock / reinforced target |
| VanA / VanB | Target swap / altered binding motif |
| ESBL | Enzyme badge on gram-negative rod |
| KPC / NDM | High-threat carbapenemase sigil |
| Efflux pump | Pump/vent icon pushing drug away |
| Porin loss | Closed gate / blocked entry symbol |

## **Clinical Status / Lab / Toxicity Icons**

### **Role**

These should support decision-making and monitoring.

### **Style**

Use simple UI symbols, not characters.

Examples: 

- Renal adjustment
- Hepatic issue
- Therapeutic drug monitoring
- Allergy
- Nephrotoxicity
- Myopathy
- Cytopenia
- Cultures pending
- Blood cultures positive
- Source control needed
- De-escalation opportunity

## **Art Tiers**

## **Tier 1:  Detailed Character Art**

Used for: 

- drug selection cards
- tutorial panels
- case introductions
- “about this drug” screens
- promotional images

These can be more detailed and expressive.

## **Tier 2:  Gameplay Sprites**

Used for: 

- active tactical screen
- case board
- simplified drug/bug representation

These should be simpler and readable at small size.

## **Tier 3:  Icons and Badges**

Used for: 

- susceptibility
- resistance
- lab status
- toxicity
- dosing alerts
- clinical modifiers

These must be very simple and immediately readable.

## **Color System**

## **Core Color Meanings**

| **Meaning** | **Color Direction** |
| --- | --- |
| Susceptibility | Blue |
| Resistance | Red / purple |
| Antibiotic action | Blue / white / gold |
| Broad-spectrum / heavy therapy | Deep blue / steel / gold |
| Warning / monitoring | Amber |

## **Important Color Rule**

Color should never be random.

The same color should mean the same kind of thing across the interface.

- gold/yellow color

### **Avoid**

- full monster body
- arms/legs
- excessive facial expression
- making it too cute

## **Vancomycin**

### **Current Direction**

Blue-white anti-hero guardian / binding sentinel.

### **Should Communicate**

- binds target
- heavy and deliberate
- powerful but requires monitoring
- serious therapeutic presence

### **Visual Motifs**

- clasping hands
- bound target
- blue-white armor
- amber monitoring accent
- stern visor or eyes

### **Avoid**

- scary monster design
- villain silhouette
- carefree hero tone
- generic pill/IV representation

## **Cefazolin**

### **Current Direction**

Beta-lactam wall-breaker / siege knight.

### **Should Communicate**

- beta-lactam
- cell-wall disruption
- reliable frontline therapy
- active wall-breaking

### **Visual Motifs**

- hammer
- mace
- battering ram
- cracked brick wall
- impact burst
- white-blue-gold armor

### **Avoid**

- shield-only defender concept
- ambiguous weapon
- weapon appearing broken unless intentionally representing irreversible binding
- generic knight without mechanism cue

## **Educational Accuracy Guardrails**

Visual metaphors can simplify mechanisms, but they should not teach the wrong concept.

## **Acceptable Metaphors**

- Cefazolin breaking bacterial wall construction
- Vancomycin binding a cell-wall precursor target
- Daptomycin disrupting membrane integrity after calcium activation
- Beta-lactamase disabling beta-lactams
- MRSA showing altered target or resistance marker

## **Risky Metaphors**

- Making all antibiotics appear to work by smashing walls
- Making resistance look like generic “extra HP”
- Making monitoring-heavy drugs look risk-free
- Making bacteria so cute that the clinical threat disappears
- Making drug classes visually interchangeable

# **Filled Example:  Cefazolin**

## **1. Asset Identity**

**Asset name:** Cefazolin

**Asset type:** Drug

**Educational role:** Represents beta-lactam cell-wall disruption and reliable MSSA-directed therapy when appropriate.

**Where it appears:** Drug card, gameplay token, tutorial, case action animation

## **2. Clinical Meaning**

**Clinical concept represented:** First-generation cephalosporin, commonly used for MSSA and surgical prophylaxis scenarios.

**Mechanism or key clinical idea:** Beta-lactam inhibition of bacterial cell-wall synthesis through penicillin-binding proteins.

**What learners should remember:** Cefazolin is a focused, dependable beta-lactam option for susceptible organisms like MSSA.

**Common misconception to avoid:** Do not imply cefazolin covers MRSA.

## **3. Visual Role**

**Personality level:** High

**Suggested role fantasy:** Frontline wall-breaker / siege knight

**Emotional tone:** Heroic, dependable, forceful

**Should feel like:** A disciplined antimicrobial breacher that breaks bacterial wall integrity.

**Should not feel like:** A passive shield-bearer, generic paladin, or vague fantasy knight.

## **4. Visual Metaphor**

**Primary metaphor:** Wall-breaking siege hammer.

**Secondary metaphor:** Clean, reliable frontline agent.

**Required visual cues:** Hammer or ram, cracked brick wall, impact burst.

**Optional visual cues:** White-blue-gold armor, beta-lactam crest, confident eyes.

**Forbidden visual cues:** Pill, tablet, IV bag, syringe, literal chemical structure as the main image.

## **5. Shape and Silhouette**

**Overall shape:** Compact armored humanoid with large hammer.

**Silhouette goal:** Readable as “hero with wall-breaking weapon.”

**Major props or body features:** Hammer, cracked wall target, gold/blue armor.

**Small-size readability concern:** Hammer and cracked wall need to remain recognizable.

## **6. Color Palette**

**Primary colors:** White, blue, gold

**Secondary colors:** Amber impact sparks, gray wall fragments

**Avoid these colors:** Dominant red/purple, unless showing resistance.

**Color meaning:** White/blue/gold = clean, clinical, reliable beta-lactam action. Amber = impact.

## **7. Educational Guardrails**

**Must accurately imply:** Cell-wall synthesis disruption.

**Must not imply:** MRSA coverage or toxin suppression.

**Resistance interaction to consider:** Fails if resistance mechanism prevents binding or beta-lactam activity.

**Monitoring/toxicity cue needed?** No major cue needed for base cefazolin sprite.

## **8. Version Requirements**

**Detailed character art:** Yes

**Gameplay sprite:** Yes

**Small icon/badge:** Yes

**Animation needed?** Attack/impact animation later.

**Suggested animation:** Hammer swings down, bacterial wall cracks, fragments scatter.

# **Filled Example:  MSSA**

## **1. Asset Identity**

**Asset name:** MSSA

**Asset type:** Bacteria

**Educational role:** Represents methicillin-susceptible *Staphylococcus aureus*.

**Where it appears:** Enemy token, case organism display, susceptibility screen

## **2. Clinical Meaning**

**Clinical concept represented:** A susceptible *S. aureus* isolate, often appropriately treated with anti-staphylococcal beta-lactams depending on syndrome and patient factors.

**Mechanism or key clinical idea:** Susceptibility matters. MSSA should visually differ from MRSA.

**What learners should remember:** MSSA and MRSA are related but should drive different therapy choices.

**Common misconception to avoid:** Do not make MSSA look resistant or armored.

## **3. Visual Role**

**Personality level:** Low

**Suggested role fantasy:** Organism-like threat token

**Emotional tone:** Clinically threatening but not monstrous

**Should feel like:** A recognizable gold cocci cluster.

**Should not feel like:** A cute monster mascot or villain character.

## **4. Visual Metaphor**

**Primary metaphor:** Golden grape-like cocci cluster.

**Secondary metaphor:** Susceptible organism marker.

**Required visual cues:** Gold clustered cocci, blue S badge.

**Optional visual cues:** Subtle aura, mild shadow, tiny implied eyes.

**Forbidden visual cues:** Arms, legs, teeth, claws, big angry face.

## **5. Shape and Silhouette**

**Overall shape:** Clustered round cocci.

**Silhouette goal:** Readable as staph-like cluster.

**Major props or body features:** Blue susceptibility badge.

**Small-size readability concern:** Badge must remain readable, or be simplified to a blue dot/ring at small size.

## **6. Color Palette**

**Primary colors:** Gold, yellow, orange

**Secondary colors:** Blue susceptibility marker

**Avoid these colors:** Dominant red/purple unless representing resistance.

**Color meaning:** Gold = *aureus*. Blue S = susceptible.

## **7. Educational Guardrails**

**Must accurately imply:** MSSA, not MRSA.

**Must not imply:** Resistance, special armor, or beta-lactam failure.

**Resistance interaction to consider:** MRSA variant should use same base morphology but altered marker/armor.

**Monitoring/toxicity cue needed?** No.

## **8. Version Requirements**

**Detailed character art:** Optional

**Gameplay sprite:** Yes

**Small icon/badge:** Yes

**Animation needed?** Idle pulse only.

**Suggested animation:** Subtle cluster wobble or infection pulse.

It should look clinically recognizable, mildly threatening, and game-readable, but not cute, monstrous, or humanoid. Use a limited palette, crisp pixel-art edges, transparent background, centered sprite, and no extra scenery.
